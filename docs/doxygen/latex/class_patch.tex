\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Contains the information of a local mosquito population. 

The population is divided into four types\+: juveniles (J), males (M), virgin (unmated) females (V) and mated females (F). These are then subdivided into six genotypes (in this order)\+: WW, WD, DD, DR, WR and RR composed of a wild-\/type allele (W), a drive-\/type allele (D) and a resistant-\/type allele (R). Juveniles are also subdivided into age groups, ordered from oldest (0 days left to eclosion) to youngest ({\ttfamily max\+\_\+dev} -\/ 1 days left). The population can carry out life-\/processes and interface with other classes to introduce gene drive mosquitoes into the \doxylink{class_patch}{Patch}, carry out dispersal in and out of the \doxylink{class_patch}{Patch} and aestivate.
